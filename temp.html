Overview

This project is about a novel jailbreak method that enhances jailbreak efficiency through self-reflecting on contextual feedback, leveraging unique vulnerabilities across different models and scenarios.

What did we introduce? a multi-agent LLM system named RedAgent that generates context-aware jailbreak prompts.

What did we find? By generating context-aware jailbreak prompts, we successfully jailbreak most black-box LLMs in just five queries, improving the efficiency of existing red teaming methods by two times. Additionally, RedAgent can jailbreak customized LLM applications more efficiently, e.g., we discover 60 severe vulnerabilities of GPTs with only two queries per vulnerability.

Results


RedAgent' Comparison with Baselines

We compare our method with existing jailbreak methods, including Direct Attack, PAIR, TAP, PAP and GPTFuzzer on AdvBench Subset. The table below shows the comparison of ASR(Attack Success Rate) and ANQ (Average number of successful jailbreak queries) across various jailbreak methods. The experimental results demonstrate that our method achieved similar or even higher attack success rates with significantly fewer queries than existing approaches.


Ablation Study

We conducted ablation experiments on two key hyperparameters: Memory Capacity, and Memory Tags, aiming to evaluate the impact of different parameter settings on the ASR and ANQ of our solution. Experimental results show that a memory capacity of 25, and a default setting of the memory tag (category, question, prompt, strategy, score) can achieve a good balance between ASR and ANQ.

Ethics and Disclosure

In this study, we adhered to ethical standards to ensure safety and privacy. Our experiments were conducted using platforms provided officially or through open-source models deployed in a closed environment. We did not disseminate any harmful or illicit content to the public or others. The datasets we employed were obtained from public repositories and did not contain any personal information. The main objective of this study is to highlight potential vulnerabilities in LLMs, especially given the rapid pace of their adoption. Moreover, we have responsibly disclosed our findings to OpenAI and Meta.
